The difference between 'the government is' and 'the government are' is a political statement in English. In British English, collective nouns take plural verbs. In American English, singular. The same grammar rule — applied differently — signals your nationality, your education, and which version of English was dominant in your school. Grammar is cultural identity.

🏥 Stage 4: Error Correction Hospital

Each sentence has one error (underlined in red). Tap the error word to correct it!

Patient 1 — Subject-Verb Agreement
The committee are meeting tomorrow. (American English context)
✓ Healed: 'are' → 'is' (American English: collective nouns take singular verbs)
Patient 2 — Article Error
She is a honest person who never lies.
✓ Healed: 'a' → 'an' ('honest' starts with a vowel sound /ɒ/, so 'an' is correct)
Patient 3 — Tense Error
By the time she arrived, he already left.
✓ Healed: 'left' → 'had left' (past perfect needed — leaving happened before arriving)
Patient 4 — Preposition Error
She is very good in Mathematics.
✓ Healed: 'in' → 'at' (correct idiom: good at a subject)
Patient 5 — Determiner Error
He had little friends, so he was never lonely.
✓ Healed: 'little' → 'a few' ('little' is for uncountable nouns; 'friends' is countable → 'a few')
