📊 Stage 4: Histogram vs Bar Graph

These two graphs look similar but are fundamentally different. Classify each example correctly:

Graph A: Number of students scoring in ranges 0-20, 20-40, 40-60, 60-80, 80-100. No gaps between bars.
Graph B: Number of students playing cricket, football, basketball, chess. Bars have gaps between them.
Graph C: Annual rainfall in Guwahati, Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai. Each city is separate.
Key distinction: Histogram = continuous data (no gaps, bars touch). Bar graph = categorical/discrete data (gaps between bars).
