🔧 Stage 2: Format Assembler — Formal Letter

A formal complaint letter has a strict structure. Tap each element to place it in the correct position. Wrong order = marks deducted!

Body paragraph — explaining the complaint in detail
Sender's address + Date
Complimentary close + Signature (Yours faithfully)
Concluding paragraph — requesting action
Receiver's address + Subject line + Salutation (Dear Sir/Ma'am)
→ Letter structure (tap elements above in correct order)
Common errors: Missing date · Missing subject line · Writing 'Dear Friend' instead of 'Dear Sir/Madam' · Ending with 'Yours sincerely' when you don't know the name (should be 'Yours faithfully')

🔦 Stage 4: Opening Line Laboratory

Article topic: 'The Importance of Sports in School'. Rank these opening lines — tap each to reveal how a reader would rate it and why.

"Sports is important for students. It helps them stay healthy and fit."
⭐☆☆☆☆
Weak — states the obvious with no hook, no specificity, no reason to keep reading.
"In 1992, Derek Redmond tore his hamstring mid-race at the Olympics, hobbled to the finish line supported by his father — and received a standing ovation. Sports doesn't just build bodies. It reveals character."
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐
Excellent — specific story hook, unexpected, leads directly to the thesis, creates emotional engagement before the argument begins.
"According to various studies, sports participation has been found to correlate positively with academic performance in secondary school students."
⭐⭐⭐☆☆
Average — accurate and formal, but dry. 'Various studies' is vague. Works for a report but not an engaging article.
"Can running a race teach you more than a textbook? Every student who has ever stood at a starting line knows the answer."
⭐⭐⭐⭐☆
Strong — rhetorical question creates engagement, the second sentence builds on it. Could be even stronger with a specific example.
Principle: The best opening lines do three things simultaneously: (1) surprise or intrigue the reader, (2) establish the article's central claim, (3) make the reader want to read sentence two.
✂️ Stage 5: 50-Word Notice — The Compression Challenge

Read the full description below. Now write a notice in maximum 50 words that includes ALL required elements.

The Cultural Committee of Kendriya Vidyalaya, Guwahati, has organised a Bihu Dance and Music Festival. The event will take place on 14th April 2025, from 10:00 AM to 4:00 PM, in the School Auditorium. All students from Classes 6 to 12 are invited to participate or attend. Students who wish to participate must register with the Cultural Secretary, Ms. Priya Sharma, by 10th April. There will be prizes for winners in solo dance, group dance, and instrument performance categories.
Required elements: ✓ School name · ✓ 'NOTICE' heading · ✓ Date · ✓ Event name · ✓ Timing & venue · ✓ How to register · ✓ Secretary's signature
Word count: 0 / 50
SAMPLE ANSWER (48 words):
KENDRIYA VIDYALAYA, GUWAHATI
NOTICE
Date: 5 April 2025
BIHU FESTIVAL — 14 APRIL
All students (Classes 6–12) are invited to the Bihu Dance and Music Festival on 14 April, 10 AM–4 PM, School Auditorium. Register for solo/group dance or instrument by 10 April.
— Priya Sharma, Cultural Secretary
