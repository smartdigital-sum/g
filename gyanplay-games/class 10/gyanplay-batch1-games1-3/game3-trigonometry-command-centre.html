The word 'sine' comes from a chain of mistranslations spanning Arabic, Latin, and Sanskrit over 1,400 years. Indian mathematician Aryabhata called it 'ardha-jya'. Arabs shortened it to 'jiba'. European translators misread Arabic script and wrote 'sinus'. The word in every student's textbook is a 1,400-year-old mistranslation of an Indian concept.
