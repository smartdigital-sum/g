The word 'should' versus 'must' is the difference between advice and a legal obligation. 'You should wear a seatbelt' is advice. 'You must wear a seatbelt' is law. Entire legal codes, medical instructions, and ethical debates hinge on which modal verb is used. Modals are where grammar becomes power.

Stage 2: Conditional Sentences
Type 0: Scientific Truth
If water boils, it turns to steam. (Always true)
Type 1: Real Future
If it rains, I will take an umbrella. (Likely)
Type 2: Unreal Present
If I were rich, I would travel. (Hypothetical)
Type 3: Unreal Past
If she had studied, she would have passed. (Regret)
