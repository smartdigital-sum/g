A 2016 study showed that students who learned to identify logical fallacies scored 34% better at reading comprehension. The ability to spot a bad argument is the same skill as constructing a good one — and both are more rare and valuable than any factual knowledge. Thinking IS a learnable skill.
Stage 1: Argument Anatomy
The Toulmin Model of argumentation:
CLAIM
What you argue (your position)
EVIDENCE
The facts/data that support your claim
WARRANT
Why the evidence supports the claim (the reasoning)
REBUTTAL
Counter-argument and your response to it
Identify the part: "Studies show that students who learn fallacies score 34% better on reading tests."

Stage 2: Logical Fallacy Spotter
Ad Hominem
Attack the person, not the argument
False Binary
Only two options presented
Slippery Slope
Assumes extreme consequence
Appeal to Authority
Expert said so = must be true
Bandwagon
Everyone does it
Straw Man
Misrepresent opponent's position
Example: "My opponent wants to raise taxes, so he wants to destroy the economy and make everyone poor."

Stage 3: Debate Builder
Should the Brahmaputra Dam be opposed?
China is building a dam upstream that could affect water flow to Assam.
Select Your Claim
The dam should be opposed due to flooding risks downstream.
The dam is China's sovereign right and should not be opposed.
Select Your Evidence
Assam already faces annual floods affecting 2 million people.
International rivers require cross-border cooperation.
Select Your Warrant
Reducing water flow during monsoons increases flood risk.
Countries have the right to develop their own resources.

Stage 4: Handle Counter-Arguments
Your Argument: "Social media should have an age limit of 16."
Evidence: Studies link social media use to teen anxiety.
Counter-Argument: "Teens will find ways around age limits, making them ineffective."
Select the best rebuttal:
Rebuttal A: While some teens may bypass limits, age verification still reduces overall exposure for the most vulnerable younger teens, which is the primary goal.
Rebuttal B: You're wrong and don't understand the issue at all.
Rebuttal C: Many experts agree with my position.
